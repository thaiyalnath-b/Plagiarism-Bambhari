About US

BRI Plagiarism is a research-driven platform developed under Bambhari Research Institute to support academic integrity, originality, and responsible content creation. As digital content continues to expand across education, research, and online publishing, verifying the authenticity of written work has become more important than ever.

What is Plagiarism?

Understanding academic integrity and content originality

Types of Plagiarism

Direct Plagiarism

Copying text word-for-word without attribution

Example: Copying a paragraph from a source without quotes or citation

Mosaic Plagiarism

Changing words while keeping original structure

Example: Replacing synonyms in a sentence while maintaining the same structure

Idea Plagiarism

Presenting someone's ideas as your own

Example: Using a unique research methodology without credit

Self-Plagiarism

Reusing your own previous work without citation

Example: Submitting the same paper to multiple classes
